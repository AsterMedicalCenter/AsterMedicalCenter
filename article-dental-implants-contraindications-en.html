When Should You Not Get Dental Implants?

June 2026 • Department of Oral Surgery and Implantology

Dental implants are a wonderful solution for replacing missing teeth, but they are not suitable for everyone! In some cases, they may cause serious complications. Learn about the situations where implants should be postponed or avoided altogether.

⚠️ 5 Cases Where Dental Implants Are Not Recommended:

1. If You Suffer from Severe Osteoporosis

Reason: Lack of jaw bone density may lead to the failure of the implant to heal and integrate.

Alternative Solution: Fitting a temporary bridge or removable denture until bone health improves.

2. Uncontrolled Diabetes Patients

Risk: High blood sugar slows bone fusion around the implant and increases the risk of infection.

Safe Level: Cumulative HbA1c must be less than 7.7 before implantation.

3. Heavy Smokers (More than 20 cigarettes a day)

Impact: Nicotine reduces blood flow in the blood vessels of the gums and hinders healing.

Solution: Quit smoking 3 months before implantation or choose classic alternatives such as bridges.

4. Patients Who Underwent Radiation Therapy in the Jaw Area

Risk: Radiation exposure can cause a complex medical condition called "osteonecrosis" after implantation.

Safe Alternative: A specially designed denture under medical supervision for oncology patients.

5. Presence of an Active Infection in the Gums or Jaw

Why? Active bacteria may transfer directly to the open implant area and cause immediate failure.

Required Steps: Treat gum disease and periodontal pockets first, then wait 3 to 6 months before implantation.

Other Cases Requiring Careful and Customized Evaluation:

  • Pregnant Women: It is always preferable to postpone elective implantation until after a safe delivery.
  • Heart Patients: They mandatorily require written approval from their treating physician.
  • Teenagers and Children: Implantation is strictly prohibited for them, and one must wait for the jaw bone growth to be completely finished.

Free Advice from Aster Medical Center Doctor:

  • Do not rush into the decision to implant without a thorough comprehensive examination (3D CT scan and blood tests).
  • Choose a doctor specialized in complex implant cases if you fall into the high-risk categories mentioned above.
  • Adhering to the doctor's post-implant instructions (daily cleaning and periodic visits) is more important than the surgery itself.
